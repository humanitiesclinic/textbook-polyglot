Remark 1.3.3. Agglutination vs. Fusion.

Some languages, like Turkish and Finnish, are agglutinative, meaning morphemes line up visibly in sequence. Others, like English and Spanish, show fusion, where morpheme boundaries blur through sound changes. Turkish evlerimden (from our houses) shows clear morpheme boundaries: ev-ler-im-den (house-plural-our-from), while English went fuses the past tense morpheme obscurely with the root.
