While all human languages are fundamentally spoken or signed, most modern languages also have writing systems—conventional ways of representing language visually. Different writing systems encode language in different ways: some represent individual sounds (alphabetic), some represent syllables (syllabic), and some represent words or morphemes (logographic). Understanding these systems is essential for polyglot linguists, as scripts reveal how different languages conceive of and organize their sound and meaning systems.
