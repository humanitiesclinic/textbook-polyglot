Not every pronunciation difference signals a phoneme difference. Allophones are variant pronunciations of the same phoneme that do not change meaning. English speakers pronounce the [l] in light differently from the [l] in milk (one is “clear l,” the other “dark l”), but these differences don’t matter—[laɪt] and [mɪlk] are understood as containing the same phoneme /l/.
